The digestive system
Briefly about the main

The digestive system, which begins with the mouth and ends with the anus, is responsible for taking food, breaking it down into nutrients (a process called digestion), absorbing nutrients into the bloodstream, and expelling the uneaten food.

Organs of the digestive system:

  • Pancreas
  • Liver
  • Gallbladder
  • Salivary glands
Digestive tract:
  • Mouth
  • Throat and esophagus
  • Stomach
  • Small intestine
  • Large intestine
  • Rectum and anus
The digestive system is sometimes called the gastrointestinal tract, but neither name fully describes the functions or components of the system. Organs of the digestive system also produce clotting factors and hormones unrelated to digestion, help remove toxic substances from the blood, and chemically modify (metabolize) drugs.

Experts recognize the powerful connection between the digestive system and the brain. For example, psychological factors have a significant impact on bowel contractions, secretion of digestive enzymes and other digestive system functions. Even the susceptibility to infections, which leads to various digestive system disorders, is largely influenced by the brain. The digestive system, in turn, affects the brain. For example, prolonged or recurrent illnesses such as irritable bowel syndrome, ulcerative colitis and other pain-inducing conditions affect emotions, behavior and daily activities. Such a reciprocal relationship has been called the gut-brain axis.
