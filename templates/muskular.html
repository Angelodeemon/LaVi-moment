The musculoskeletal system

The musculoskeletal system is a functional combination of the bones of the skeleton, their joints (joints and synarthroses) and somatic muscles with auxiliary apparatus, which, through nervous regulation of locomotion, maintain posture, facial expressions and other motor actions, along with other organ systems, form the human body.

Musculoskeletal system functions:
  • Supportive: it supports the entire body; soft tissues and organs are attached to the bones
  • Motor: a system of levers with movable joints driven by muscles
  • Protective: they form cavities for the vital organs - the spinal canal for the spinal cord; the cranial box for the brain; the thoracic cavity for the heart and lungs; the pelvic bones for the genitourinary system
  • Mineral metabolism: bones are depots for mineral salts: phosphorus, calcium, iron, copper; they regulate the constancy of the mineral composition of the body's internal environment
  • Hematopoietic function: bone marrow hematopoietic stem cells form blood and immune system cells
